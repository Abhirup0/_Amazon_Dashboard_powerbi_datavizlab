Intro + Project Overview & Dashboard

⏱ 2.5 Minutes  ·  Slides 1–3

Good morning everyone. I'm Abhirup Karmakar, and today I'll be presenting our project, Amazon Sales Performance: Data-Driven Insights for Growth and Optimization.

In today's competitive e-commerce landscape, businesses generate massive volumes of data every day — but raw data alone does not create value. The real advantage comes from transforming that data into meaningful insights that support faster decisions, improved efficiency, and strategic growth.

That is exactly what motivated this project.

Our objective was to design a centralized, interactive, one-page business intelligence dashboard using Amazon sales data that enables decision-makers to monitor performance, identify trends, and act proactively.

This project focuses on four key goals:

01 · Build & Centralize Bring multiple business metrics into a single unified dashboard, eliminating fragmented reporting and creating one reliable source of truth.
02 · Monitor Real-Time KPIs Track critical indicators such as sales performance, order volume, and average delivery time, allowing dynamic performance monitoring.
03 · Identify Key Business Drivers Uncover what drives profitability, which customer segments perform best, and where operational inefficiencies or losses exist.
04 · Empower Action The ultimate purpose of analytics is not just insight, but decision-making. This dashboard supports faster, smarter, and more profitable business actions.

Moving to the dashboard overview —

We designed this as a one-page executive intelligence solution, where high-level decision-makers can understand business performance in just a few seconds.

The dashboard integrates several analytical layers.

At the top, we present core KPIs such as Estimated Sales, Product Units, Order Volume, and Shipping Cost, giving an immediate performance snapshot.

It also includes dynamic year-wise filters from 2012 to 2015, enabling comparative trend analysis over time.

For deeper insights, we incorporated sales segmentation analysis, order-priority distribution, profit analysis by shipping mode, and customer-wise profitability tracking.

Additionally, a global map visualization provides geographic performance insights, helping analyze logistics patterns and regional sales distribution.

What makes this dashboard valuable is that it combines KPI monitoring, segment analysis, logistics intelligence, and geographic visualization into a single analytical framework — designed not just for reporting, but for decision support.

In short, this project demonstrates how business intelligence can transform sales data into actionable insights for optimization, efficiency, and growth.

Thank you — now I'll move forward to the next part of the presentation.

Let us begin with Shipping Performance.

This analysis focuses on the relationship between delivery methods and profit generation.

One of the strongest insights revealed by the dashboard is that Standard Class shipping generates the highest profit, approximately 0.89 million, significantly outperforming all other shipping modes.

This creates a very clear profitability pattern: as shipping speed increases, profit margins decline.

The reason behind this is that faster delivery options generally involve higher fulfillment and logistics costs, which reduce overall profitability despite offering speed advantages.

In contrast, Standard Class, being more cost-efficient, delivers significantly stronger margins and emerges as the most profitable shipping model.

Another important observation is that despite this emphasis on cost efficiency, the business still maintains an average delivery time of approximately 4 days, which indicates operational efficiency remains strong.

So strategically, balancing customer service with cost-efficient shipping can improve both profitability and fulfillment performance.

In simple terms, not all fast delivery creates high profit — in this case, smarter shipping creates higher value.

Now moving to Top Customers: The Profit Drivers.

This section shifts focus from operational profitability to customer-driven profitability.

The analysis shows that profit is heavily concentrated among a relatively small group of high-value clients.

Customer A — 1.25M The single largest profit contributor.
Customer B — 980K  ·  Customer C — 890K Followed by several other major clients contributing substantial revenue.

What this tells us is that a significant portion of profit is driven by a limited number of loyal, high-value customers.

This is a critical business insight — because when revenue is concentrated among key customers, those relationships become strategically important, not just financially valuable.

It means customer retention, loyalty programs, personalized engagement, and premium relationship management should prioritize these clients to secure long-term base revenue.

So if the previous slide answered which shipping strategy drives profitability, this slide answers which customers sustain that profitability.

Together, these insights connect operational strategy with customer strategy — showing that profit optimization depends both on how we deliver and who we serve.

Loss-Making Products + Global Distribution + Conclusion

⏱ 2.5 Minutes  ·  Final Slides

Thank you, Shayak. Now I will present the final analytical section — covering Loss-Making Products, Global Sales Distribution, and the Key Insights & Conclusion, which together focus on risk identification, growth opportunities, and strategic takeaways.

Let me begin with Loss-Making Products.

While revenue growth is important, sustainable business performance depends equally on protecting profit.

This analysis helps identify revenue leaks — specific product lines that consistently generate negative profit margins.

The dashboard shows that several products are operating at recurring losses, with the highest recorded loss reaching approximately 8.9 thousand on a single product line.

This is a significant finding — because even when sales volume appears strong, unprofitable products can quietly erode margins and weaken overall performance.

That is why this insight signals immediate action requirements. From a business perspective, these products should be reviewed through:

Action Options Pricing analysis, cost optimization, supply chain adjustments, or, if necessary, product discontinuation.

Because the goal is not simply to grow revenue, but to ensure that growth remains profitable.

Now moving to Global Sales Distribution.

This section shifts from internal risk analysis to external market opportunity.

The geographic visualization shows that sales are distributed across multiple international regions, with strong high-density clusters indicating established market dominance in core regions.

This confirms that the business already has a strong global footprint.

However, an equally important insight comes from the lower-density regions. These emerging markets represent expansion opportunities where penetration remains relatively low, but growth potential remains significant.

Strategically, this suggests the business can pursue growth not only by strengthening mature markets, but also by expanding selectively into underdeveloped regions.

So this analysis balances market strength with future opportunity.

Now finally, moving to Key Insights and Conclusion.

This project generated four major strategic findings:

01 · Growth & Audience Revenue is scaling steadily, led primarily by the Consumer Segment, which remains the dominant sales driver.
02 · Expansion Opportunity Global analysis reveals strong potential to expand into low-density, emerging markets.
03 · Logistics & Loyalty Standard Shipping emerged as the most profitable shipping model, while a small group of high-value customers drives a major share of profit.
04 · Profitability Risk Certain products consistently generate losses and require immediate management attention.

When these insights are viewed together, they show that business optimization is not driven by a single factor, but by the combination of customer intelligence, operational efficiency, geographic strategy, and profitability control.

And that is the core contribution of this project — transforming Amazon sales data into actionable business intelligence for smarter decisions, stronger growth, and sustainable optimization.

Thank you.
